LOAD TEST ON VILLIERS AIR COOLED PETROL ENGINE 

AIM:

To conduct load test on 3 HP Petrol/Kerosene engine and to plot B.H.P. Vs F.C., S.F.C., Air / Fuel ratio, Brake mean effective pressure, Brake Torque, Volumetric efficiency, Brake thermal efficiency etc.,

APPARATUS:



Engine, Tachometer, Stop watch etc.,



TECHNICAL DETAILS:



Speed	=  3000 rpm; 	 
Power=  3BHP;
Number of Cylinders =  One
Bore	=  70mm;         
Stroke	=  66.7 mm;	        
Make    =  Villiers
Method of Cooling : Air Cooling;         
Loading = Belt Brake Type
Brake Drum diameter =  19.42 cm;       
Belt thickness = 6 mm
Orifice diameter = 16 mm;.                   
cd = 0.6.



DESCRIPTION:

The engine is a 4 stroke, 3bhp air-cooled vertical cylinder single cylinder Petrol/Kerosene Engine. The engine is provided with a belt brake dynamometer. The loading on the shaft takes place by providing weights on the spring balance. Fuel is supplied from fuel tank through a three way valve. For obtaining fuel consumption, fuel will be drawn from burette provided by cutting of the fuel from the fuel tank. Air is supplied to the engine through the air filter and air tank. The quantity of air drawn into the cylinder can be measured by noting down the Manometric head from U- tube water manometer. Exhaust gas temperature measuring dial gauge is provided on the panel. Basically this engine works on the Kerosene. But as the self ignition temperature of Kerosene is more, for starting the engine Petrol is to be used.

PROCEDURE:

Initially Petrol has to be supplied to the engine. Engine (flywheel) has to be rotated with the help of the rope. The engine speed has to be adjusted to the rated speed. Soon after the engine has attained the rated speed fuel need to be switched to Kerosene from Petrol. Sufficient time has to be given for the engine to stabilize. Manometer reading, time required for consuming 20 cc of fuel, speed of the engine has to be noted down. Engine has to be loaded against different loads and respective readings are to be recorded. Care should be taken that the water is sent through the brake drum while loading the engine. Repeat the experiment for different loads. At every load ensure that the rated speed is maintained.
