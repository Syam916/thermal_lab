HEAT BALENCE SHEET

AIM:

To draw Heat balance sheet


APPARATUS:

10BHP Kirloskar Diesel Engine, Tachometer, Stop watch etc.,


TECHNICAL DETAILS:


No. of Cylinders : 2;                       
Method of Cooling:  Water Cooling;                          
Speed: 1500 rpm  
Bore Diameter :  87.5 mm;          
: 110 mm;      
Brake Power =10 BHP at 1500 rpm or 7.36 kW
Compression Ratio:  17.5  :  1;     
Brake Drum Diameter: 24.5 cm.;                       
Cd = 0.5 


DESCRIPTION:


The engine is a 4-stroke, 10 BHP water cooled, vertical, two cylinders Kirloskar Diesel Engine. The cylinders 
are placed in-line, The engine is provided with a belt brake dynamo-meter.  Fuel is supplied from fuel tank 
through a three way valve.  For obtaining fuel consumption the fuel will be drawn from burette provided by 
cutting off the fuel from the fuel tank.  The quantity of air drawn into the cylinder may be measured by 
noting down the Manometric head from U-tube water manometer.  Two dial gauges are provided on the panel to 
measure cooling water outlet temperature and exhaust gas temperature at engine.  As the engine is water 
cooled water supply is also provided to the brake drum to cool the brake drum while loading the engine.


PROCEDURE:


The fuel level and lubricating oil level are checked.  The decompression laver is lifted so that the exhaust
 value will be opened.  The fly wheel is rotated with the help of cranking handle. When the fly wheel has 
 attained sufficient speed the decompression lever is placed horizontally so that the exhaust value will 
 be in normal operation and the engine starts functioning.  When the engine has warmed up cooling water 
  supplied to the cylinder jacket.  The engine speed is adjusted to the rated speed of 1500 rpm. by 
  adjusting the fuel supply with the help of fuel control knob provided on the panel.  Sufficient time 
  is allowed for the engine to stabilize.  At no load conditions the engine cooling water inlet and outlet 
  temperatures are measured.  The quantity of cooling water supplied per minute is noted.  The exhaust gas 
  temperature near the engine and the ambient air temperature are noted.  The time taken for 20 cc of fuel 
  consumed and the Manometric head are noted. Then the engine is loaded and the above observations are made 
  along with the load.  For every set of readings about 20 minutes time is allowed for the engine to 
  stabilize. Procedure repeated for four sets.
